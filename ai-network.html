The core question

“We changed something. Did it help — or did the world just move?”

Most teams answer that by eyeballing a graph. The rigorous answer is an experiment: compare what happened against a control that didn’t get the change, so you separate your effect from everything else going on. That’s difference-in-differences — and it’s the difference between a real result and a coincidence.

I bring that scientific rigour to network and infrastructure telemetry, with AI doing the heavy lifting on the data — and the judgment to know when a number can be trusted.
