The way in — automate the right work

Most automation fails because people automate the wrong things — a bot shop builds whatever you point at and you get no return. The hard part is choosing what's worth it. That's the judgment I owned running network automation for a Tier-1 telco.

So the first step is simple: I find one automation actually worth doing, build it through the system, and hand that system to your team. Use-case judgment plus a machine you keep — not bot-building.

The handover is a pass/fail — that's the guarantee

Judgment doesn't transfer just because a board exists. So you don't take my word that your team can run it — they prove it:

  1. We pick one real recurring output.
  2. I run cycle 1, narrating every decision.
  3. Your owner runs cycle 2 — I only step in at the gates.
  4. Your owner runs cycle 3 alone, from the runbook.
  5. Handover completes only if cycle 3 passes the acceptance checklist.
