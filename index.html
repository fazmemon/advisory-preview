The real problem

AI projects don’t fail on the tech. They fail on the choice.

Anyone can spin up a chatbot now — that part’s a commodity. Businesses get burned because they automate the wrong thing, can’t trust the output, or end up depending on a consultant forever. The scarce skill is judgment: picking the one workflow that’s worth it, and making it stick.

The most common first answer

An assistant on your own documents — that never makes things up.

For a lot of businesses the first thing worth automating is the same: the hours lost hunting through policies, guidelines and handbooks. A team member asks; it answers from your material, shows the source, and says “that’s not in our documents” rather than guessing. Built and working — and the bit a regulated business needs before it can say yes to AI.
